Yes. Dubai allows foreign nationals to purchase freehold property in designated areas — which now includes most of the popular residential communities. There are no residency requirements for ownership, though banks will apply their own lending criteria for mortgage applications.

Plan for approximately 7–8% on top of the purchase price: 4% DLD transfer fee, agency commission (typically 2%), a registration fee, and minor administrative charges. We provide a detailed cost breakdown before you sign anything.

A cash transaction can complete in as little as 2–4 weeks. Mortgage transactions typically take 6–10 weeks, with the bulk of that time sitting with the bank's valuation and approval process. We manage every step and keep you informed at each milestone.

Gross yields in Dubai typically range from 5–9% depending on location and property type. Furnished short-term rental properties in central areas can achieve higher, though they require active management. We'll share comparable rental data for any property you're considering before you commit.
